The Author

Words are the only things
I know how to keep.

Fida Rasul Baig is an Indian bestselling author known for writing that cuts to the bone — stories that live in the space between what people feel and what they dare to say. Born on January 18, the habit of writing started quietly, privately, as a way of surviving the noise inside.

Two books published. Thread explores social anxiety, depression, and the terrifying arithmetic of falling in love with someone who might not survive it. The Voyage of the Imaginary is an invitation to fall in love with poetry. More on the way.

Beyond the Books

A little about
who I actually am

There is the writing, and then there is the person doing the writing. These are some of the things that shape both.

I'm a January 18 person. I believe mornings are better quiet, stories are better long, and most conversations worth having start after midnight. I live in India, perpetually either reading something, writing something, or wishing I'd written something down when I had the chance.

Fun Facts

01
Writing began as a private habit

Not a career plan, not ambition — just a way of surviving the noise inside. The decision to publish came later, after realising the stories were too big to keep alone.

02
Born on January 18

A January soul: quietly ambitious, deeply introspective, impossible to rush when there's something worth thinking about.

03
Writes about real emotions

Social anxiety, depression, love, loss — the themes in my books aren't invented. They come from the complicated, honest world of being human.

04
Coffee over everything

Black coffee is non-negotiable. It's how sentences start, how late nights survive, and how first drafts happen.
